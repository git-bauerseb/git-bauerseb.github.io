Some facts I learned about that will always be burnt in my memory:

  1. Fourier transform: \[ \hat{f}(\zeta) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x)e^{-2\pi i x \zeta} \text{dx} \] and fast multiplication of polynomials in $$\mathcal{O}(n\log{}n)$$
  2. Heap data-structure
  3. Recursive-descent/Pratt parsing
  4. Blockchains and the transformation of finance through crypto currencies
  5. Rule of Bayes: \[ P(x | y) = \frac{P(y | x) \cdot P(x)}{P(y)} \]
  6. The proof that there are infinitely many primes by considering \[ p_1 p_2 \cdot \dots \cdot p_n + 1 \]
  7. Backpropagation algorithm
  8. Irrationality of \[\sqrt{2}\]
  9. The trick to evaluate \[ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2} \text{dx} = \sqrt{\pi} \] via polar coordinates.
  10. Axons in the brain can be as long as one meter.
  11. Nothing is faster than light, but light cannot escape black holes.
  12. Black-holes don't exist forever
  13. There are \[ \frac{8! \cdot 3^8 \cdot 12! \cdot 2^{12}}{3 \cdot 2 \cdot 2} \] \[ = \text{43.252.003.274.489.856.000} \] different configurations for a rubik's cube.
